## I. STATEMENT OF LIABILITY

### A. Clear Negligence Established

Our client was lawfully operating a motor vehicle in an established traffic lane on Wayne Newton Boulevard when the adverse driver negligently performed an unsafe lane change, directly striking our client's vehicle. The liability in this matter is **clear, undisputed, and supported by official police investigation.**

**Basis for Liability:**

1. **Police Report Findings:** The Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department (LVMPD) investigation (Report #LLV25010008341) determined that the adverse driver was **at fault** for violation of Nevada Revised Statutes § 484B.227 (Unsafe Lane Change).

2. **No Negligence on Client:** Our client made no traffic violations. **No citation was issued to Mr. Richko**—he maintained his established lane and possessed the right-of-way throughout the incident.

3. **Legal Standard:** NRS 484B.227 requires drivers to ensure a lane change is safe and will not impede the normal and prudent operation of any traffic that the operator is aware of or should be aware of. The adverse driver failed this statutory duty.

4. **Statutory Violation = Negligence Per Se:** The other driver's violation of NRS 484B.227 constitutes negligence per se, eliminating any need to establish duty and breach through general negligence principles.

### B. Causation & Damages Flow

The adverse driver's negligent unsafe lane change was the direct and proximate cause of the incident, resulting in bodily injury to our client and reasonable and necessary medical expenses. The causal chain is straightforward and uncontroverted.
