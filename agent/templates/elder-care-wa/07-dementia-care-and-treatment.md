## VII. Dementia Care & Treatment

### FDA-Approved Medications
| Medication | Type | Stage | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|-------|
| **Donepezil** (Aricept) | Cholinesterase inhibitor | Mild–severe | Most commonly prescribed; once daily |
| **Rivastigmine** (Exelon) | Cholinesterase inhibitor | Mild–moderate | Available as patch (better GI tolerance) |
| **Galantamine** (Razadyne) | Cholinesterase inhibitor | Mild–moderate | Twice daily; GI side effects common |
| **Memantine** (Namenda) | NMDA antagonist | Moderate–severe | Often combined with donepezil |
| **Lecanemab** (Leqembi) | Anti-amyloid antibody | Early/mild | IV infusion q2 weeks; slows decline ~27%; ARIA risk |
| **Donanemab** (Kisunla) | Anti-amyloid antibody | Early/mild | IV infusion; may allow treatment completion; ARIA monitoring |

- Cholinesterase inhibitors: **modest symptomatic benefit**, do not stop progression
- Anti-amyloid therapies: require amyloid PET or CSF confirmation; **ARIA** (brain swelling/bleeding) monitoring with MRI
- No approved disease-modifying treatment for FTD or vascular dementia

### Behavioral & Psychological Symptoms (BPSD)
**Non-pharmacological approaches (FIRST LINE):**
- Structured routines, consistent caregivers
- Redirect and distract rather than correct or argue
- Music therapy, art therapy, pet therapy
- Reduce environmental stimulation (noise, clutter)
- Ensure adequate lighting (reduces sundowning)
- Rule out pain, infection (UTI), constipation, medication side effects as behavioral triggers

**Pharmacological management (when non-pharm fails):**
- **SSRIs** (citalopram, sertraline): for depression, anxiety, agitation
- **Trazodone**: for sleep disturbance, mild agitation
- **Atypical antipsychotics** (risperidone, quetiapine): for severe agitation/psychosis — **FDA black box warning** for increased mortality in elderly dementia patients
- **Avoid** benzodiazepines (fall risk, paradoxical agitation) and anticholinergics (worsen cognition)
- **Lewy body**: quetiapine preferred if antipsychotic needed; **avoid haloperidol** and other typicals

### Caregiver Support
- **Caregiver burnout**: affects 40–70% of dementia caregivers; screen for depression
- WA resources: **Family Caregiver Support Program** (FCSP) through Area Agency on Aging
- **Adult day programs**: structured activities, respite for caregiver, social engagement
- **In-home care**: ranges from companion care ($25–35/hr) to skilled nursing
- **Support groups**: Alzheimer's Association 24/7 helpline **1-800-272-3900**
- **Respite care**: temporary relief for caregivers — in-home, adult day, or facility-based

### Care Transitions
| Level | When Appropriate | Typical Cost (WA) |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **In-home care** | Mild–moderate; caregiver available | $25–40/hour |
| **Adult family home** | Moderate; needs daily assistance, small setting (2–6 residents) | $4,000–8,000/month |
| **Assisted living / memory care** | Moderate–severe; needs structured environment | $5,000–10,000/month |
| **Skilled nursing facility** | Severe; complex medical needs | $10,000–15,000/month |
| **Hospice** | FAST 7c or 6-month prognosis | Covered by Medicare Part A |

### Hospice Eligibility for Dementia
- **FAST stage 7c** (unable to ambulate independently, limited speech, incontinent) OR
- Significant functional decline with comorbidities
- Recurrent infections (aspiration pneumonia, UTI), weight loss, skin breakdown
- Must be **certified by two physicians** as having ≤6 months prognosis if disease runs normal course
- Medicare covers: nursing visits, medications for comfort, medical equipment, respite, bereavement support
- Can receive hospice **at home, in facility, or in hospice house**

### Driving & Safety
- Assess driving safety at **every visit** once cognitive decline is identified
- WA DOL can require re-examination based on physician report (RCW 46.20.305)
- Document conversations about driving cessation
- Safety modifications: door alarms, stove shutoffs, medication lockboxes, GPS tracking devices
