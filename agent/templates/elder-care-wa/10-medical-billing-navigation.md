## X. Medical Billing Navigation

### Understanding Medicare Billing Documents
| Document | What It Is | Action Needed |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Medicare Summary Notice (MSN)** | Quarterly summary of claims processed | Review for errors; NOT a bill |
| **Explanation of Benefits (EOB)** | From Medicare Advantage or supplement insurer | Compare with provider bills |
| **Provider bill/statement** | Actual amount owed | Pay only after MSN/EOB confirms amount |

### Reading an MSN / EOB
- **Billed amount**: what the provider charged (often inflated)
- **Medicare-approved amount**: what Medicare considers reasonable
- **Medicare paid**: 80% of approved amount (Part B) after deductible
- **You may be billed**: 20% coinsurance + deductible (or what Medigap covers)
- **Check for errors**: wrong diagnosis code, duplicate charges, services not received

### Medicare Appeals Process (5 Levels)
| Level | Filed With | Deadline | Decision Time |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **1. Redetermination** | Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC) | 120 days from MSN | 60 days |
| **2. Reconsideration** | Qualified Independent Contractor (QIC) | 180 days from Level 1 | 60 days |
| **3. ALJ Hearing** | Office of Medicare Hearings and Appeals | 60 days from Level 2 | 90 days |
| **4. Medicare Appeals Council** | Departmental Appeals Board | 60 days from Level 3 | 90 days |
| **5. Federal District Court** | U.S. District Court | 60 days from Level 4 | Varies |

- For **Part C/D** (Medicare Advantage): separate appeals process through the plan, then independent review
- **Expedited review**: available for urgent situations (within 72 hours)

### Prior Authorization
- Required by Medicare Advantage plans for many services (surgeries, imaging, specialty drugs, DME)
- Original Medicare: limited prior auth requirements (mainly certain DME and procedures)
- If denied: request **expedited appeal** for urgent medical needs
- Provider should submit prior auth; patient can follow up if delayed
- **CMS prior auth reforms (2024+)**: MA plans must respond within 7 days (standard) or 72 hours (expedited)

### Balance Billing Protections
- **Medicare providers** who accept assignment cannot bill above Medicare-approved amount
- **Non-participating providers**: can charge up to **15% above** Medicare fee schedule ("limiting charge")
- **Opted-out providers**: can charge any amount (must sign private contract)
- **No Surprises Act** (2022): protects against surprise bills for emergency services and out-of-network providers at in-network facilities

### Medicare Savings Programs (MSPs)
Help low-income beneficiaries with Medicare premiums and cost-sharing:

| Program | Income Limit (2025, single) | What It Covers |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **QMB** (Qualified Medicare Beneficiary) | ≤100% FPL ($1,255/mo) | Part A & B premiums, deductibles, coinsurance |
| **SLMB** (Specified Low-Income Beneficiary) | 100–120% FPL | Part B premium only |
| **QI** (Qualifying Individual) | 120–135% FPL | Part B premium only |

- Asset limits: **$9,430** single / **$14,130** couple (2025)
- Apply through WA DSHS (Department of Social and Health Services)
- QMB enrollment also qualifies for **Extra Help** (Part D Low-Income Subsidy)

### Extra Help (Part D Low-Income Subsidy)
- Helps pay Part D premiums, deductibles, and copays
- **Full subsidy**: income ≤135% FPL and limited resources
- **Partial subsidy**: income 135–150% FPL
- Apply through Social Security or WA DSHS
- Copays under full subsidy: **$0–$4.50** for generics, **$0–$11.20** for brand-name (2025)

### Practical Tips
- Always verify a provider is **Medicare-enrolled** before services
- Request an **Advance Beneficiary Notice (ABN)** before any service Medicare might not cover
- Keep a billing log: date of service, provider, billed amount, MSN reference, payment
- Use **1-800-MEDICARE** (1-800-633-4227) for billing questions
- WA SHIBA (Statewide Health Insurance Benefits Advisors): free Medicare counseling — **1-800-562-6900**
