## IX. Medicare & Long-Term Care

### Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) — The 100-Day Rule
- Medicare Part A covers SNF **only after** a qualifying 3-day inpatient hospital stay
- Coverage: up to **100 days** per benefit period
  - Days 1–20: **$0** coinsurance (Medicare pays full)
  - Days 21–100: **$204.50/day** coinsurance (2025) — patient or supplemental insurance pays
  - Days 101+: **no Medicare coverage** — patient pays full cost or Medicaid if eligible
- Must be **medically necessary skilled care** (nursing, PT, OT, speech therapy)
- "Observation status" (outpatient) does NOT count toward the 3-day stay — ask hospital to convert to inpatient if possible
- Medicare Advantage plans may waive the 3-day stay requirement for SNF (check plan rules)

### Home Health Services
- Medicare covers **intermittent** skilled nursing and therapy at home
- Requirements: homebound, physician-ordered plan of care, need skilled services
- **No prior hospitalization** required (unlike SNF)
- Covers: skilled nursing, PT, OT, speech therapy, medical social work, home health aide (limited)
- Does NOT cover: 24-hour care, meal delivery, homemaker services, personal care only
- No copay for home health services under Original Medicare

### Hospice Care (Part A)
- For terminal illness with **≤6 months** prognosis (certified by 2 physicians)
- Covers: nursing, medications for comfort, DME, respite care (up to 5 days), counseling, bereavement
- Patient agrees to **forgo curative treatment** for terminal condition (can still treat other conditions)
- Can receive hospice in any setting: home, facility, hospice house
- **Copays**: $5 max for outpatient drugs, 5% of Medicare-approved amount for respite
- Can revoke hospice at any time and return to curative treatment
- Can re-enroll in hospice if eligible

### The Long-Term Care Gap
Medicare covers **medical** care but NOT **custodial** care. This creates a critical gap:

| Need | Medicare Covers? | Who Pays? |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| Skilled nursing post-hospital | Yes (100 days max) | Medicare + Medigap/MA |
| Ongoing nursing home (custodial) | **No** | Private pay → Medicaid |
| Assisted living / memory care | **No** | Private pay → Medicaid waiver |
| Adult family home | **No** | Private pay → Medicaid COPES |
| In-home personal care (bathing, dressing) | **No** | Private pay → Medicaid |
| Long-term care insurance | N/A | Policyholder pays premiums |

### Planning for the Gap
- **Average WA nursing home cost**: $10,000–15,000/month
- **Average duration of need**: ~3 years for women, ~2 years for men
- Funding strategies:
  1. **Private pay** from savings, investments, home equity
  2. **Long-term care insurance** (best purchased age 50–60)
  3. **WA Cares Fund** (state LTC benefit — see Section XIV)
  4. **Medicaid** (after spend-down to eligibility — see Section XI)
  5. **VA Aid & Attendance** (for qualifying veterans/spouses)
  6. **Life insurance conversion** (accelerated death benefit or life settlement)
  7. **Reverse mortgage** (HECM) — access home equity for care costs

### Medicare Advantage vs. Original Medicare for Elders
| Factor | Original Medicare + Medigap | Medicare Advantage |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **Provider choice** | Any Medicare provider nationwide | Network restrictions |
| **Out-of-pocket max** | No cap (Medigap helps) | Required cap (**$8,850** in-network, 2025) |
| **Extras** | None | Often includes dental, vision, hearing, OTC |
| **Prior authorization** | Rarely | Common — can delay care |
| **Best for** | Frequent travelers, complex medical needs, specialists | Healthy, local, budget-conscious |
| **SNF coverage** | Requires 3-day stay | May waive 3-day requirement |
