## XII. Elder Financial Planning

### Social Security Optimization
| Claiming Age | Benefit Adjustment |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **62** (earliest) | ~70% of full benefit (permanently reduced) |
| **67** (FRA for those born 1960+) | 100% of full benefit |
| **70** (maximum) | ~124% of full benefit (8%/year delayed credits) |

- **Spousal benefit**: up to 50% of higher earner's FRA benefit; no delayed credits past FRA
- **Survivor benefit**: up to 100% of deceased spouse's benefit (including delayed credits)
- **Strategy for couples**: higher earner delays to 70 to maximize survivor benefit; lower earner may claim earlier
- **Earnings test** (before FRA): benefits reduced $1 for every $2 earned above **$22,320** (2025)
- Social Security is **taxable**: up to 85% of benefits taxed if combined income exceeds $34,000 (single) / $44,000 (joint)

### Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
- Must begin at age **73** (for those born 1951–1959) or **75** (born 1960+) — per SECURE Act 2.0
- Applies to: Traditional IRA, 401(k), 403(b), 457(b), other pre-tax retirement accounts
- **Roth IRAs**: no RMDs during owner's lifetime
- **Roth 401(k)**: no RMDs starting 2024 (SECURE Act 2.0)
- Penalty for missed RMD: **25%** of amount not withdrawn (reduced to 10% if corrected within 2 years)
- RMD calculated: prior year-end balance ÷ IRS life expectancy factor (Uniform Lifetime Table)

### Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)
- Direct transfer from IRA to qualified charity, up to **$105,000/year** (2025, indexed)
- Counts toward RMD but **excluded from taxable income**
- Must be age **70½+** and made directly from IRA (not from employer plan)
- Cannot go to donor-advised fund or private foundation
- **Best for**: seniors who don't itemize deductions — get tax benefit without itemizing
- One-time QCD to split-interest entity (CGA or CRAT) up to **$53,000** (2025)

### Gifting Strategies
- **Annual gift exclusion**: **$18,000** per recipient per year (2025) — no gift tax return required
- **Married couples**: can gift **$36,000** per recipient (gift-splitting)
- **529 superfunding**: contribute up to 5 years of annual exclusion at once (**$90,000** single / **$180,000** married) to education savings
- Gifts remove assets from taxable estate (important for WA estate tax planning)
- **No WA gift tax** — only federal gift tax reporting above annual exclusion
- Caution: gifts within **60 months** of Medicaid application trigger penalty period

### Step-Up in Basis at Death
- Inherited assets receive **fair market value basis** at date of death (IRC §1014)
- **Community property advantage**: BOTH halves of community property get stepped-up basis at first spouse's death
- Example: home purchased for $200K, worth $800K at death → new basis $800K (no capital gains if sold immediately)
- **Appreciated stock**: often better to hold until death for step-up rather than gift during lifetime
- No step-up for assets in **irrevocable trusts** (if grantor doesn't retain sufficient interest)

### Long-Term Care Insurance
- Covers custodial care costs that Medicare does not
- **Best purchased age 50–60** (premiums increase significantly with age; health underwriting)
- Key policy features:
  - **Daily/monthly benefit**: $150–300/day typical
  - **Benefit period**: 2–5 years (or unlimited)
  - **Elimination period**: 30–90 days before benefits begin
  - **Inflation protection**: 3% compound recommended for those under 70
- **Hybrid policies** (life insurance + LTC rider): growing in popularity, guaranteed benefits
- **WA Cares Fund** may reduce but doesn't replace private LTC insurance need (see Section XIV)

### Investment Considerations for Elders
- **Shift toward income and preservation**: reduce equity concentration as care needs increase
- Maintain **1–2 years of expenses** in liquid assets (cash, short-term bonds)
- Consider **TIPS** (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities) for inflation protection
- **Annuities**: can provide guaranteed income but watch for high fees and surrender charges
- **Reverse mortgage (HECM)**: access home equity without selling; must be 62+, must continue to pay taxes/insurance
- Review portfolio annually with focus on income needs, tax efficiency, and care cost reserves
