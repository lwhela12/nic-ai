## I. WA Estate Planning Fundamentals

### Community Property Rules
- Washington is a **community property state** — all property acquired during marriage is owned 50/50
- Separate property: inherited assets, pre-marriage assets, gifts to one spouse
- Commingling separate + community funds can convert separate property to community
- At death, decedent can dispose of their **50% community share** plus all separate property
- **Full step-up in basis** for both halves of community property at first spouse's death (IRC §1014(b)(6))

### Core Estate Planning Documents
| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Revocable Living Trust** | Avoids probate, manages assets during incapacity, distributes at death |
| **Pour-Over Will** | Catches assets not titled in trust; names guardian for minor children |
| **Durable Power of Attorney** | Financial decision-making during incapacity |
| **Healthcare Directive** | Medical decision-making, end-of-life wishes |
| **HIPAA Authorization** | Allows agents to access medical records |
| **Community Property Agreement** | Converts all property to community; passes to survivor without probate |

### Washington Probate Overview
- Governed by **Title 11 RCW** (Probate and Trust Law)
- **Nonintervention powers** (RCW 11.68): most WA estates administered without court supervision
- Probate threshold: no small estate affidavit for real property; personal property **≤ $100,000** qualifies (RCW 11.62)
- **4-month creditor claim period** after notice publication (RCW 11.40.051)
- **TEDRA** (Trust and Estate Dispute Resolution Act, RCW 11.96A): allows binding nonjudicial agreements among beneficiaries

### Community Property Agreement (CPA)
- Written agreement that all property is community and passes to survivor at death
- **Avoids probate entirely** for married couples — no trust needed in many cases
- Must be signed by both spouses, notarized recommended
- Caution: bypasses will provisions; not ideal for blended families or estate-tax-exposed estates
- Can be combined with a trust for tax planning or incapacity management

### Trust Administration After Death
1. Obtain death certificate (order **10–15 certified copies**)
2. Notify beneficiaries per RCW 11.97.010 (20-day notice requirement)
3. Obtain **EIN** for the trust (becomes irrevocable at death)
4. Inventory and retitle assets; manage distributions per trust terms
5. File final individual tax return + trust/estate returns (Form 1041)
6. File WA estate tax return if estate exceeds **$2,193,000** (2025) / **$3,076,000** (2026 projected)
