## XI. WA Medicaid & Long-Term Care

### Apple Health (WA Medicaid) — Long-Term Care Programs
Washington's Medicaid long-term care is administered by **DSHS/Aging and Long-Term Support Administration (ALTSA)**. Key programs:

| Program | Setting | Description |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **COPES** (Community Options Program Entry System) | Home, adult family home, assisted living | Waiver program for nursing-home-eligible individuals to receive care in community |
| **Medicaid Personal Care (MPC)** | Home | Personal care services for those not nursing-home-eligible |
| **Nursing Facility Medicaid** | Nursing home | Full Medicaid coverage for nursing home care |
| **Roads to Community Living** | Transition from facility | Helps move from nursing home back to community setting |

### Eligibility — Financial Requirements (2025)
**Income:**
- Applicant: ≤ **300% of SSI** = **$2,829/month** (2025)
- If over limit: can use **Miller Trust** (Qualified Income Trust) to qualify
- Community spouse: **no income limit** — spouse keeps their own income

**Assets/Resources:**
- Applicant: ≤ **$2,000** in countable resources
- Community spouse: keeps **CSRA** (Community Spouse Resource Allowance) = **$154,140** max (2025), minimum **$30,828**
- **Exempt assets**: primary home (equity ≤ $713,000), one vehicle, personal property, prepaid burial, term life insurance

### Spend-Down Strategies (Legal)
- **Pay off mortgage/home improvements** — converts countable cash to exempt home equity
- **Purchase exempt assets**: prepaid burial plan, new vehicle, home repairs, personal items
- **Pay existing debts**: credit cards, loans, medical bills
- **Establish Miller Trust** if income exceeds limit
- **Caregiver agreement**: pay family caregiver for documented care at fair market rate
- **Purchase annuity** (Medicaid-compliant): must be irrevocable, non-assignable, actuarially sound, name state as remainder beneficiary

### Look-Back Period
- **60-month (5-year) look-back** for all asset transfers
- DSHS reviews all transfers made within 60 months before application
- Gifts, transfers for less than fair market value → **penalty period** (ineligibility)
- Penalty calculation: total uncompensated transfers ÷ average private nursing home rate (~$13,500/month in WA) = months of ineligibility
- **Exceptions**: transfers to spouse, disabled child, trust for sole benefit of disabled person under 65, home transfers to certain family members

### Spousal Protections
- **CSRA**: community spouse keeps up to **$154,140** in assets (2025)
- **MMNA** (Minimum Monthly Maintenance Needs Allowance): community spouse can keep **$2,555–$3,853.50/month** from institutionalized spouse's income (2025)
- **Court-ordered support**: can petition court for higher MMNA if needed
- Home is exempt while community spouse (or dependent) resides there
- Community spouse can retain all jointly-owned property up to CSRA without penalty

### Home Equity & Estate Recovery
- Home exempt during lifetime if: community spouse resides there, OR applicant intends to return, OR equity ≤ $713,000
- After death: WA **Medicaid estate recovery** (RCW 43.20B.080) — state seeks reimbursement from estate
- Recovery from: probate estate, nonprobate assets (including trusts, joint accounts, TOD deeds)
- **Protections**: no recovery while surviving spouse alive, while child under 21 or disabled in home
- **Hardship waiver**: available in limited circumstances

### Application Process
1. **Functional eligibility**: assessed by DSHS social worker using **CARE** tool (Comprehensive Assessment Reporting Evaluation)
2. **Financial eligibility**: income and asset verification
3. Apply through DSHS — can apply online, by phone, or in person
4. Processing time: **45 days** for most applications, 90 days if disability determination needed
5. Once approved: case manager develops care plan and authorizes services
