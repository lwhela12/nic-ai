## V. Beneficiary Designations & Nonprobate Transfers

### Nonprobate Assets (Pass Outside the Will/Trust)
These assets transfer by **beneficiary designation** or **title**, not through probate:
- Life insurance policies
- Retirement accounts (IRA, 401(k), 403(b), pension)
- Payable-on-death (POD) bank accounts
- Transfer-on-death (TOD) brokerage accounts
- TOD deeds for real property (RCW 64.80)
- Joint tenancy with right of survivorship (JTWROS)
- Community property agreements

### Common Pitfalls
- **Outdated beneficiaries**: ex-spouse still listed after divorce — WA statute revokes ex-spouse designations by operation of law (RCW 11.07.010) but ERISA plans (401k) may override state law
- **Estate as beneficiary on IRA/401(k)**: loses stretch, triggers income acceleration, may expose to estate creditors
- **No contingent beneficiary**: if primary predeceases, account falls to estate
- **Beneficiary conflicts with trust**: will says "all to trust" but IRA beneficiary names individual — **beneficiary designation wins**
- **Per stirpes vs. per capita**: specify distribution method if beneficiary predeceases

### Transfer-on-Death (TOD) Deeds — Washington
- Authorized under **RCW 64.80** (Uniform Real Property Transfer on Death Act)
- Deed records during lifetime; property transfers to named beneficiary at death without probate
- Grantor retains full control, can sell, mortgage, or revoke the TOD deed at any time
- Beneficiary has **no interest** until grantor's death
- Revocation: record a new TOD deed or a revocation instrument
- Caution: property may still be subject to WA estate tax and Medicaid estate recovery

### Special Needs Trusts (SNTs)
- **Third-party SNT**: funded by someone other than the disabled beneficiary (e.g., parent's estate)
  - No payback requirement to Medicaid at death
  - Can be created in parent's will or revocable trust
- **First-party (d)(4)(A) SNT**: funded with beneficiary's own assets (e.g., inheritance, settlement)
  - Must include Medicaid payback provision
  - Must be established before age 65
  - Used to preserve Medicaid/SSI eligibility
- **ABLE accounts** (RCW 43.330.460): tax-advantaged savings up to **$18,000/year** (2025) without affecting SSI/Medicaid; total balance up to **$100,000** excluded from SSI resource limit

### Retirement Account Beneficiary Rules (SECURE Act 2.0)
| Beneficiary Type | Distribution Rule |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Surviving spouse** | Roll over to own IRA; use deceased's RMD schedule; or 10-year rule |
| **Eligible designated beneficiary** (minor child, disabled, chronically ill, ≤10 years younger) | Stretch over life expectancy |
| **Non-eligible designated beneficiary** (adult children, most others) | **10-year rule** — full distribution by end of year 10 |
| **No designated beneficiary** | 5-year rule (if death before RBD) or ghost life expectancy |

### Review Checklist
- [ ] All retirement accounts have named primary + contingent beneficiaries
- [ ] Life insurance beneficiaries current and coordinated with estate plan
- [ ] Bank/brokerage accounts have POD/TOD designations or are titled in trust
- [ ] Real property: consider TOD deed or trust titling
- [ ] Special needs beneficiary identified → direct to SNT, not outright
- [ ] Review beneficiary designations **every 2–3 years** or after major life events
