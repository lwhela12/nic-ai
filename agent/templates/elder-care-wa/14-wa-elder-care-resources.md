## XIV. WA Elder Care Resources

### WA Cares Fund (Long-Term Care Benefit)
- **State-run LTC insurance program** — first in the nation (RCW 50B.04)
- Funded by **0.58% payroll tax** on employee wages (no employer match)
- **Lifetime benefit**: up to **$36,500** (indexed, as of 2026) for qualifying long-term care services
- Eligibility: vested workers (paid premiums for 10 years without gap > 5 years, OR paid premiums in 3 of last 6 years)
- Benefits begin when individual needs assistance with **3+ ADLs** (activities of daily living)
- Covers: in-home care, adult family home, assisted living, memory care, home modifications, caregiver training
- **Opt-out**: available only for those who had private LTC insurance by Nov 1, 2021 (permanent opt-out)
- **Exempt populations**: temporary workers with visas, military spouses, out-of-state workers

### Adult Family Homes (AFH)
- Licensed homes providing care for **2–6 residents** in a residential setting
- Regulated by WA DSHS under **RCW 70.128**
- Must be licensed; inspected regularly; caregiver-to-resident ratios specified
- Can provide: personal care, medication management, dementia care, skilled nursing coordination
- **Cost**: $4,000–8,000/month private pay; COPES Medicaid covers for eligible individuals
- Search licensed AFHs: WA DSHS provider search tool

### Assisted Living & Memory Care
- **Assisted living**: licensed under **RCW 18.20** (Boarding Home Act)
- Provides housing, meals, personal care, medication management, activities
- **Memory care**: specialized units within assisted living or standalone; secured environment, trained staff
- Must have individual negotiated service agreement (NSA) for each resident
- **Cost**: $5,000–10,000+/month; some accept Medicaid (COPES) but limited availability

### WA Long-Term Care Partnership Program
- Coordinates private LTC insurance with Medicaid
- Qualifying partnership policies allow dollar-for-dollar **asset disregard** for Medicaid eligibility
- Example: if partnership policy pays out $200,000, individual can keep $200,000 in assets above Medicaid limit
- Encourages purchasing private LTC insurance to delay/reduce Medicaid dependency
- Policy must meet specific partnership criteria (inflation protection, state approval)

### Elder Abuse Reporting & Protection
- **Mandatory reporting**: healthcare providers, social workers, law enforcement, long-term care staff
- Report to: **DSHS Adult Protective Services (APS)** — **1-866-END-HARM** (1-866-363-4276)
- Types: physical, emotional, sexual, neglect, abandonment, financial exploitation, self-neglect
- **Vulnerable Adult Protection Act** (RCW 74.34): defines vulnerable adults and protective services
- Financial exploitation is the **most common** form — monitor for sudden financial changes, new "friends," unusual transactions
- WA Attorney General's office handles complex financial exploitation cases

### Veterans Benefits for Elder Care
- **VA Aid & Attendance**: pension supplement for veterans/surviving spouses needing help with ADLs
  - Up to **$2,431/month** for veteran, **$1,564/month** for surviving spouse (2025)
  - Must have 90+ days active service, at least 1 day during wartime
  - Income and asset limits apply (net worth ≤ **$155,356** including assets, 2025)
  - **3-year look-back** on asset transfers (similar to Medicaid)
- **VA community care**: may cover some medical services through community providers
- **VA home care programs**: homemaker/home health aide, respite care, adult day health
- Apply through VA regional office or accredited Veterans Service Organization (VSO)
