## VI. Dementia Types & Stages

### Major Dementia Types

#### Alzheimer's Disease (~60–70% of cases)
- Progressive memory loss, especially recent/short-term memory
- Amyloid plaques and tau tangles in the brain
- Gradual onset over years; average survival **8–12 years** from diagnosis
- Early signs: repeating questions, misplacing items, getting lost in familiar places
- Genetic risk factors: APOE-e4 allele (risk factor, not deterministic)

#### Vascular Dementia (~10–20%)
- Caused by reduced blood flow to the brain (strokes, small vessel disease)
- Often **stepwise decline** — sudden worsening after vascular events, then plateaus
- Executive function (planning, organizing) affected early; memory may be relatively preserved
- Risk factors: hypertension, diabetes, smoking, atrial fibrillation
- Prevention: aggressive cardiovascular risk management

#### Lewy Body Dementia (~5–10%)
- Abnormal **alpha-synuclein** protein deposits in brain cells
- Hallmark features: **visual hallucinations**, fluctuating cognition, parkinsonism (tremor, rigidity, slow movement)
- **REM sleep behavior disorder** often precedes cognitive symptoms
- **Avoid antipsychotics** (especially typical) — severe sensitivity, can be fatal
- Overlaps with Parkinson's disease dementia (timing of motor vs. cognitive symptoms differs)

#### Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD) (~5–10%)
- Affects frontal and temporal lobes; often younger onset (**age 45–65**)
- **Behavioral variant**: personality changes, disinhibition, apathy, loss of empathy
- **Language variants**: progressive aphasia (word-finding, speech fluency)
- Memory relatively preserved early (unlike Alzheimer's)
- More **genetic** component than other dementias (~30–50% familial)
- No approved medications; management focuses on behavioral strategies

### Cognitive Assessment Tools
| Tool | Score Range | Key Thresholds |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **MMSE** (Mini-Mental State Exam) | 0–30 | ≥24 normal; 18–23 mild; 10–17 moderate; <10 severe |
| **MoCA** (Montreal Cognitive Assessment) | 0–30 | ≥26 normal; more sensitive to early/mild impairment |
| **CDR** (Clinical Dementia Rating) | 0–3 | 0=normal; 0.5=MCI; 1=mild; 2=moderate; 3=severe |
| **FAST** (Functional Assessment Staging) | 1–7 | Stages 1–7 with substages; correlates to care needs |

### Staging: Mild → Moderate → Severe
**Mild (early)**
- Forgetfulness, word-finding difficulty, misplacing items
- Mostly independent in ADLs; may need help with finances, medications
- Legal capacity likely still intact — **ideal time for estate planning**
- Can still participate meaningfully in care decisions

**Moderate (middle)**
- Significant memory gaps, confusion about time/place
- Needs help with ADLs: dressing, bathing, meal preparation
- Behavioral symptoms: agitation, wandering, sundowning, sleep disruption
- **Longest stage** — may last several years
- Safety concerns increase: driving, cooking, fall risk

**Severe (late)**
- Minimal verbal communication; may not recognize family
- Dependent for all ADLs; incontinence common
- Swallowing difficulties → aspiration risk → weight loss
- Increased vulnerability to infections (pneumonia, UTI)
- Hospice-appropriate when **FAST stage 7c** or significant functional decline

### Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)
- Cognitive decline beyond normal aging but **not severe enough for dementia diagnosis**
- ~10–15% of MCI patients progress to dementia annually
- Subtypes: amnestic MCI (memory) and non-amnestic MCI (other domains)
- Important to monitor — may be early Alzheimer's or remain stable
