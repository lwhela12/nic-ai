## II. Powers of Attorney & Healthcare Directives

### Durable Power of Attorney (DPOA) — Financial
- Governed by **Uniform Power of Attorney Act**, RCW 11.125
- Must include "durable" language to survive incapacity (RCW 11.125.060)
- Effective immediately upon signing unless "springing" (triggered by incapacity determination)
- **Hot powers** requiring express grant (RCW 11.125.240):
  - Create/amend/revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change survivorship rights
  - Delegate authority
- Agent's fiduciary duties: act in principal's best interest, avoid conflicts, keep records (RCW 11.125.150)
- Third-party acceptance: institutions must accept DPOA within **7 business days** or give written refusal with reasons (RCW 11.125.390)

### Healthcare Power of Attorney
- Part of **Healthcare Directive** under RCW 70.122 (Natural Death Act) and RCW 7.70
- Appoints healthcare agent to make medical decisions when principal cannot
- Agent authority includes: consent to/refuse treatment, access medical records, choose providers
- Two physicians must certify incapacity before agent's authority activates (unless directive specifies otherwise)

### Living Will / Advance Directive
- States wishes for life-sustaining treatment when terminally ill or permanently unconscious
- Can specify: artificial nutrition/hydration, mechanical ventilation, CPR, antibiotics
- Must be **signed, dated, and witnessed** by two adults (not the appointed agent)
- Healthcare providers who comply in good faith have legal immunity (RCW 70.122.051)

### POLST (Portable Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment)
- **Medical order** (not advance directive) — signed by physician/ARNP
- Bright green form; travels with patient across care settings
- Covers: CPR (attempt/DNR), medical interventions (full/selective/comfort), antibiotics, artificial nutrition
- Appropriate for persons with **serious illness or advanced frailty**
- Legally binding on EMS and healthcare facilities in Washington
- Review and update at each care transition or change in condition

### Guardianship vs. POA Comparison
| Factor | POA | Guardianship |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| **Timing** | Must be signed while competent | Can be imposed after incapacity |
| **Cost** | $200–500 to prepare | $5,000–15,000+ for court process |
| **Court involvement** | None | Required — petition, hearing, ongoing reporting |
| **Revocability** | Revocable while competent | Only by court order |
| **Preference** | Always preferred if possible | Last resort per UGCOPAA |

### Practical Tips
- Name **successor agents** (at least 2) in case primary agent is unavailable
- Keep originals accessible; give certified copies to agent and key institutions
- Review and update every **3–5 years** or after major life events
- Coordinate POA with trust powers — agent should also be successor trustee or work with trustee
