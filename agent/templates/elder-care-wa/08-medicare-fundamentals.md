## VIII. Medicare Fundamentals

### Medicare Parts Overview
| Part | Coverage | Premium (2025) | Notes |
|------|----------|----------------|-------|
| **Part A** (Hospital) | Inpatient hospital, SNF, hospice, some home health | **$0** for most (40+ quarters of work) | Deductible: **$1,676** per benefit period |
| **Part B** (Medical) | Outpatient, physician services, preventive care, DME | **$185/month** standard (IRMAA surcharges for higher income) | Deductible: **$257/year**, then 20% coinsurance |
| **Part C** (Medicare Advantage) | Bundled A+B through private insurer; often includes D | Varies by plan (may be $0 premium) | Must stay in-network; may include dental, vision, hearing |
| **Part D** (Prescription Drug) | Outpatient prescriptions | **$30–50/month** average | Donut hole eliminated 2025; $2,000 out-of-pocket cap |

### Enrollment Periods
| Period | When | What You Can Do |
|--------|------|----------------|
| **Initial Enrollment (IEP)** | 7-month window around 65th birthday (3 before, birth month, 3 after) | Enroll in A, B, D, or C |
| **General Enrollment (GEP)** | Jan 1 – Mar 31 each year | Late enrollment for Part B (coverage starts July 1) |
| **Annual Election (AEP)** | Oct 15 – Dec 7 | Switch between Original Medicare and MA; change Part D |
| **Open Enrollment (OEP)** | Jan 1 – Mar 31 | Switch from MA back to Original + Medigap + Part D |
| **Special Enrollment (SEP)** | Various triggers (moving, losing employer coverage) | Change plans outside standard periods |

### Late Enrollment Penalties
- **Part B**: 10% premium surcharge for **each 12 months** delayed past IEP (permanent)
- **Part D**: 1% of national base premium × months without creditable coverage (permanent)
- Exception: if covered by **employer group plan** (active employment), no penalty

### IRMAA (Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount)
- Higher-income beneficiaries pay more for Parts B and D
- Based on **modified adjusted gross income from 2 years prior** (2023 income → 2025 premiums)
- Thresholds for 2025 (single filers):
  - ≤$106,000: standard premium
  - $106,001–$133,000: +$74.00/month Part B
  - $133,001–$167,000: +$185.00/month Part B
  - Tiers continue up to >$500,000
- **Life-changing event appeal**: if income dropped due to retirement, death of spouse, divorce — file SSA-44 to use current year income

### Medigap (Medicare Supplement) Plans
- Sold by private insurers; standardized plans labeled **A through N**
- Covers Original Medicare cost-sharing (deductibles, coinsurance, copays)
- **Plan G** is most popular: covers everything except Part B deductible ($257/year)
- **Plan N**: lower premium, small copays ($20 office, $50 ER)
- **Guaranteed issue**: 6-month open enrollment starts when Part B begins (age 65+); no medical underwriting
- WA requires insurers to also offer plans during **birthday rule** window (30 days around birthday each year) — can switch Medigap plans without medical underwriting
- Cannot combine Medigap with Medicare Advantage

### What Medicare Does NOT Cover
- **Long-term custodial care** (assisted living, adult family home, ongoing nursing home)
- Dental, vision, hearing (some MA plans cover these)
- Most care outside the U.S.
- Non-medically-necessary services
