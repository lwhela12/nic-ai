## III. Guardianship & Conservatorship

### Washington's UGCOPAA Framework
- Washington adopted the **Uniform Guardianship, Conservatorship, and Other Protective Arrangements Act** (UGCOPAA) effective **January 1, 2022** (RCW 11.130)
- Replaces former Title 11.88/11.92 guardianship statutes
- Key principle: **least restrictive alternative** — court must consider less restrictive options before appointing a guardian

### Less Restrictive Alternatives (Must Be Considered First)
- Durable Power of Attorney (financial)
- Healthcare directive / healthcare POA
- Supported decision-making agreement (RCW 11.130.600–665)
- Representative payee for Social Security
- Trust with corporate trustee
- Community-based services (COPES, Medicaid waiver programs)
- Voluntary admission to care facility

### Guardianship of the Person
- **Purpose**: decision-making for personal care, residence, healthcare
- Petitioner must show: (1) respondent lacks capacity, (2) guardianship is necessary, (3) no less restrictive alternative is sufficient
- **Court Visitor** appointed to interview respondent, report to court (RCW 11.130.280)
- **Attorney for respondent** mandatory — court appoints if respondent cannot afford one
- Guardian must file **annual personal care plan** and report to court

### Conservatorship of the Estate
- **Purpose**: management of financial affairs and property
- Can be separate from or combined with guardianship of the person
- Conservator must file **inventory within 90 days** and annual financial accounting
- Bond typically required unless waived by court
- Court approval needed for: selling real property, major asset transfers, settling claims

### Filing Process (King County / WA Superior Court)
1. **Petition** filed in county where respondent resides (RCW 11.130.201)
2. Notice served on respondent personally + notice to family members
3. Court appoints **guardian ad litem** and **attorney** for respondent
4. **Hearing** — respondent has right to be present, testify, present evidence
5. Court order specifying scope of guardian/conservator authority
6. **Letters of guardianship/conservatorship** issued; recorded if real property involved

### Guardian Duties & Reporting
- Encourage respondent's self-determination and participation in decisions
- Make decisions respondent would make if able (substituted judgment standard)
- **Annual reporting**: care plan + financial accounting filed with court
- Must obtain court approval for: changing respondent's residence, major medical decisions (in some cases), restricting communication/visitation
- **Termination**: on death, restoration of capacity, or court order

### Professional vs. Family Guardians
| Factor | Family Guardian | Professional Guardian |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Cost** | Usually unpaid (may petition for fees) | **$75–200/hour** |
| **Certification** | Not required | Must be certified (RCW 11.130.010) |
| **Best for** | Simple situations, cooperative families | Complex estates, family conflict, no suitable family |
| **Bonding** | Required unless waived | Required |
| **Oversight** | Court reporting | Court reporting + certification board |

### Key Deadlines
- **14 days** notice to respondent before hearing (RCW 11.130.230)
- **90 days** for conservator to file inventory
- **Annual** reporting to court (care plan + financials)
- **60 days** to notify court of respondent's death
