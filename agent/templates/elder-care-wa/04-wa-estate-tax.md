## IV. WA Estate Tax

### Washington Estate Tax Overview
- Washington is one of **12 states** (+ DC) with a state-level estate tax
- Governed by **RCW 83.100** (Washington Estate and Transfer Tax Act)
- **No gift tax** in Washington — only estate tax at death
- **No inheritance tax** — tax is on the estate, not the beneficiary

### Exemption Thresholds
| Year | WA Exemption | Federal Exemption |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **2025** | **$2,193,000** | $13,990,000 |
| **2026** | **$3,076,000** (projected) | $7,000,000 (approx., TCJA sunset) |

- WA exemption is indexed to Seattle-area CPI; adjusted annually
- Federal exemption set to **roughly halve** on Jan 1, 2026 when TCJA sunsets
- Estates between WA and federal thresholds pay **WA tax only**

### WA Estate Tax Rates
| Taxable Estate (above exemption) | Rate |
|----------------------------------|------|
| First $1M | **10%** |
| $1M–$2M | **14%** |
| $2M–$3M | **15%** |
| $3M–$4M | **16%** |
| $4M–$6M | **18%** |
| $6M–$7M | **19%** |
| Over $7M | **20%** |

### Critical Differences from Federal
- **No portability**: WA does not allow a surviving spouse to use the deceased spouse's unused exemption (unlike federal DSUE)
- **No marital deduction "zeroing out"**: assets passing to a surviving spouse are exempt, but the first spouse's WA exemption is **lost forever** if not used
- This makes **AB trust planning** (or credit shelter trust) still relevant for WA estate tax even though it's less common federally

### AB Trust / Credit Shelter Strategy
- At first death, fund a **bypass trust (B trust)** up to the WA exemption amount
- Remainder passes to surviving spouse via marital trust (A trust) or outright
- **Preserves both spouses' WA exemptions** — can shield up to **~$6.15M** combined (2026)
- Downside: B trust assets lose the second step-up in basis at surviving spouse's death
- Weigh estate tax savings vs. capital gains tax cost on appreciated assets

### Deductions & Planning Tools
- **Marital deduction**: unlimited for assets passing to surviving spouse
- **Charitable deduction**: assets to qualified charities excluded from taxable estate
- **Family-owned business deduction** (RCW 83.100.048): up to **$2.5M** additional deduction for qualifying WA small businesses
- **Agricultural land deduction**: special valuation for qualifying farmland
- **Lifetime gifting**: no WA gift tax, so gifts reduce WA taxable estate dollar-for-dollar (but watch federal gift tax reporting > $18,000/year per recipient in 2025)

### Filing Requirements
- WA estate tax return due **9 months** after date of death (same as federal)
- Extensions available but tax payment still due at 9 months
- Filed with **WA Department of Revenue**
- Must file if gross estate exceeds WA exemption threshold (even if tax owed is $0 after deductions)
