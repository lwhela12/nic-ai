## X. Document Quality Assessment

### Required Documents Checklist
**At intake:**
- [ ] C-4 Form (Employee's Claim for Compensation)
- [ ] Employer's First Report (C-3)
- [ ] Initial medical treatment report
- [ ] Employment records (wage statements, job description)
- [ ] Signed retainer/representation agreement
- [ ] HIPAA authorizations

**During claim:**
- [ ] All ATP treatment records
- [ ] Work status reports (restrictions)
- [ ] IME reports (if conducted)
- [ ] Imaging reports (MRI, X-ray, CT)
- [ ] Physical therapy records
- [ ] Prescription records
- [ ] Vocational rehabilitation reports (if applicable)

**Before settlement/hearing:**
- [ ] PPD impairment rating report
- [ ] Complete wage records (12 weeks pre-injury)
- [ ] Benefit payment history from insurer
- [ ] All denial letters with reasons
- [ ] UR/IMR decisions
- [ ] FCE report (if conducted)
- [ ] Vocational expert opinion (if needed)

### C-4 Form Quality Checks
- Date of injury accurately stated
- Mechanism of injury clearly described
- Body parts affected properly listed
- Employer information complete and accurate
- Filed within 90-day deadline (note exact date)
- Employee signature present and dated

### Medical Records Red Flags
- Inconsistent injury descriptions across providers
- Treatment gaps without explanation
- Provider notes contradicting employee's reported symptoms
- Lack of objective findings supporting subjective complaints
- Treatment exceeding guidelines without documented justification
- History of prior claims at same body part

### Wage Statement Verification
- Verify AMW calculation using 12-week look-back
- Confirm overtime and bonus inclusion
- Check for multiple employers (combine wages)
- Verify benefit rate against state schedule
- Document any wage discrepancies with insurer
