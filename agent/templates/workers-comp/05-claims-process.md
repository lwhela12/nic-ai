## V. Claims Process & Deadlines

### Filing Requirements (NRS 616C)
- **C-4 Form (Employee's Claim for Compensation):** Must be filed within 90 days of injury or knowledge of occupational disease
- **Employer's Report (C-3):** Employer must report within 6 days of notice of injury
- **Medical documentation:** ATP must submit initial treatment report
- **90-day rule:** Failure to file C-4 within 90 days creates rebuttable presumption against compensability

### Critical Deadlines
- **90 days:** File C-4 from date of injury/knowledge
- **30 days:** Insurer must accept or deny claim after receiving C-4 (NRS 616C.065)
- **70 days:** Insurer has 70 days to complete investigation and make determination
- **1 year:** Appeal denied claim to hearings division
- **2 years:** Statute of limitations for occupational disease claims (from knowledge of disease and work connection)

### Claim Denial Process
1. Insurer issues written denial with specific reasons
2. Employee may request reconsideration
3. Appeal to Department of Administration Hearings Division
4. Hearing before hearing officer
5. Appeal to Appeals Officer
6. Judicial review in district court

### Required Forms
- **C-4:** Employee's Claim for Compensation
- **C-3:** Employer's First Report of Injury
- **C-4 Supplemental:** Additional information form
- **D-9:** Request for medical examination
- **D-16:** Request for hearing

### Documentation Best Practices
- File C-4 immediately — don't wait for the 90-day deadline
- Keep copies of all submitted forms with date stamps
- Document all communications with insurer in writing
- Request written confirmations of all verbal authorizations
- Maintain a chronological log of all claim activities
