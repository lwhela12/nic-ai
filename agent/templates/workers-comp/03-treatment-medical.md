## III. Treatment & Medical Management

### Authorized Treating Physician (ATP)
- Employee's initial choice of physician within insurer's provider network
- Change of physician requires insurer approval or DIR intervention
- ATP's opinions carry significant weight in determining benefits
- Document all referrals and ensure they are properly authorized

### Utilization Review (UR)
- Insurer may deny or modify treatment requests through UR process
- Denials must be based on evidence-based medical guidelines
- Employee has right to appeal UR denials
- Appeal process: Internal review → Independent Medical Review (IMR)
- Document all UR denials with dates and stated reasons

### Independent Medical Examination (IME)
- Insurer may request IME at any point during the claim
- Employee must attend or risk benefit suspension
- IME doctor selected and paid by insurer — inherent bias concern
- Prepare employee: answer honestly, don't minimize or exaggerate
- Obtain copy of IME report promptly
- Challenge adverse IME opinions with treating physician's records

### Treatment Authorization Process
1. ATP submits treatment request to insurer
2. Insurer has 30 days to approve/deny (NRS 616C.265)
3. If denied, appeal through UR/IMR process
4. Emergency treatment does not require pre-authorization
5. Document all delays in treatment authorization

### Common Treatment Issues
- Delays in MRI/imaging authorization
- Denial of surgical recommendations
- Premature MMI determination
- Refusal to authorize specialist referrals
- Disputes over prescription medications (opioid restrictions)
