## VI. Insurer Tactics & Defense

### Common IME Denial Tactics
- IME doctor finds "no objective findings" despite treating physician documentation
- IME concludes pre-existing condition is sole cause of symptoms
- IME determines premature MMI to cut off TTD benefits
- IME recommends against surgery despite ATP recommendation
- **Counter:** Obtain treating physician's detailed rebuttal, request independent peer review

### Surveillance Tactics
- Video surveillance of claimant during daily activities
- Social media monitoring for activity inconsistent with claimed limitations
- Sub rosa investigation through private investigators
- GPS tracking (limited legality)
- **Advise clients:** Be honest about limitations, avoid exaggeration, limit social media posting

### Functional Capacity Evaluation (FCE) Disputes
- Insurer may use FCE results to argue employee can return to work
- FCE validity depends on effort level and evaluator qualifications
- Challenge FCE if: insufficient testing time, inconsistent with medical records, evaluator bias
- Request treating physician review FCE results and provide opinion

### Delay Tactics
- Repeated requests for additional documentation
- Slow processing of treatment authorizations
- Scheduling multiple IMEs
- "Under investigation" status for extended periods
- **Counter:** Document all delays, file formal complaints with DIR, request penalties

### Benefit Termination Strategies
- Argue employee reached MMI prematurely
- Claim employee refused reasonable modified duty
- Assert employee is non-compliant with treatment
- Use IME to override treating physician opinions
- **Counter:** Maintain thorough documentation, ensure compliance with all directives

### Red Flags in Insurer Behavior
- Denial letters with vague or shifting reasons
- Repeated changes in claims adjusters
- Failure to respond to treatment authorization requests within statutory deadlines
- Pressure on employee to settle prematurely
- Contact with treating physicians without employee's knowledge
