## IX. Nevada-Specific WC Law

### Governing Statutes
- **NRS 616A:** General provisions and definitions
- **NRS 616B:** Employer obligations, insurance requirements
- **NRS 616C:** Benefits, claims process, hearings
- **NRS 616D:** Self-insured employers, associations
- **NAC 616A-D:** Administrative regulations implementing statutes

### Department of Industrial Relations (DIR)
- Oversees Nevada's WC system
- Handles complaints and disputes
- Conducts employer compliance audits
- Publishes benefit rate schedules annually
- Website: dir.nv.gov

### Key Nevada WC Principles
- **No-fault system:** Benefits paid regardless of who caused workplace injury
- **Exclusive remedy:** WC is sole remedy against employer (with limited exceptions)
- **Medical treatment:** All reasonable and necessary treatment covered
- **Choice of physician:** Employee selects from insurer's provider network
- **Anti-retaliation:** NRS 616B.012 prohibits discrimination against employees for filing claims

### Benefit Rate Schedule (Updated Annually)
- Maximum TTD rate: set by DIR based on state average weekly wage
- Minimum TTD rate: established annually
- PPD schedule: per NRS 616C.490, updated periodically
- Death benefits: NRS 616C.505

### Hearing Process
1. Request for hearing filed with Hearings Division
2. Discovery period (limited compared to civil litigation)
3. Hearing before hearing officer — less formal than court
4. Written decision issued
5. Appeal to Appeals Officer within 30 days
6. Judicial review in district court within 30 days of Appeals Officer decision

### Important Procedural Rules
- Medical evidence: ATP records given great weight
- Burden of proof: preponderance of evidence
- Attorney fees: set by regulation, not contingency-based in most cases
- Settlements: compromise and release agreements require DIR approval
- Reopening claims: within specified timeframes for new medical evidence
