## I. Claim Evaluation

### Compensability Analysis
- **AOE/COE (Arising Out of Employment / Course of Employment):** Injury must occur during and because of work duties
- **Specific injury:** Single incident with identifiable date (e.g., fall, equipment accident)
- **Occupational disease/injury:** Gradual onset due to repetitive work activities (e.g., carpal tunnel, hearing loss)
- **Cumulative trauma:** Repetitive stress injuries developing over time

### Key Compensability Factors
- Was the employee performing work duties at the time of injury?
- Did the injury occur at the workplace or during work-related travel?
- Is there a clear causal connection between work activities and the injury?
- Were there any intervening causes (personal activities, pre-existing conditions)?

### Initial Case Assessment
- **Strong claim indicators:** Witnessed incident, immediate reporting, consistent mechanism of injury, employer acknowledgment
- **Moderate claim indicators:** Delayed reporting (within 7 days), no witnesses but consistent with job duties, minor pre-existing conditions
- **Weak claim indicators:** Significant reporting delay, injury during break/personal activity, major pre-existing condition at same body part, conflicting accounts

### Employer Defenses to Watch
- Injury occurred outside scope of employment
- Employee was engaged in horseplay or willful misconduct
- Intoxication at time of injury (NRS 616C.230)
- Failure to use required safety equipment
- Pre-existing condition not aggravated by work
- Independent intervening cause
