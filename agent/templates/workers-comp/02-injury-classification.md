## II. Injury Classification

### Temporary Total Disability (TTD)
- Employee is completely unable to work during recovery
- Benefits: 66⅔% of average monthly wage (AMW), subject to state min/max
- Duration: Until employee reaches MMI or returns to work
- Maximum duration: Generally capped per NRS 616C.475

### Temporary Partial Disability (TPD)
- Employee can work in modified/light duty capacity but earning less
- Benefits: 66⅔% of difference between pre-injury and current earnings
- Applies when employee returns to work at reduced hours or lower-paying position

### Permanent Partial Disability (PPD)
- Permanent impairment remains after reaching MMI
- Rated using AMA Guides to the Evaluation of Permanent Impairment (6th Edition in Nevada)
- Benefits based on percentage of whole person impairment
- Schedule: NRS 616C.490 provides PPD benefit schedule
- Rated by treating physician or independent medical examiner

### Permanent Total Disability (PTD)
- Employee is permanently unable to engage in any gainful employment
- Benefits: 66⅔% of AMW, paid for life or until age 70 (with exceptions)
- Requires showing of total and permanent incapacity
- Vocational rehabilitation assessment typically required before PTD determination

### PPD Rating Considerations
- Impairment rating must follow AMA Guides methodology
- Consider apportionment for pre-existing conditions
- Challenge low ratings with independent evaluations
- Document all functional limitations beyond impairment rating
- Consider vocational impact beyond pure medical impairment
