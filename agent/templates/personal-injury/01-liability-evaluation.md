## I. Liability Evaluation

### Clear Liability (90-100% favorable)
- Rear-end collisions (presumption of negligence on following driver)
- Left-turn accidents where adverse ran red light or failed to yield
- DUI/impaired driver at fault
- Police report assigns fault to adverse party
- Citation issued to adverse driver
- Multiple independent witnesses corroborate client's account

### Moderate Liability (60-89% favorable)
- Disputed right-of-way with supporting evidence (dash cam, witness)
- Lane-change accidents with partial corroboration
- Parking lot accidents with unclear right-of-way
- Police report is neutral or no report filed
- Minor comparative fault arguments possible but weak

### Contested Liability (below 60%)
- Conflicting witness accounts
- Client received citation
- Multi-vehicle accidents with unclear chain of causation
- Possible comparative fault exceeding 20%
- No independent witnesses, word vs. word

### Comparative Fault Framework (Nevada NRS 41.141)
- Modified comparative fault: plaintiff recovers if **less than 50% at fault**
- Recovery reduced by plaintiff's percentage of fault
- 50% or more = total bar to recovery
- Assess comparative fault exposure early; document mitigating factors
- Common comparative fault arguments: distracted driving, failure to mitigate, seatbelt defense (limited in NV)
